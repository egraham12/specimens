Final Project

Starting with the inner ring, this visualization represents the taxanomic breakdown (phylum, class, order, and family) of some sample records from the Museum of Comparative Zoology. The sizes represent the relative number of specimens compared to the total holdings. Each arc lines up with its larger taxonomic designation (i.e. if you follow the line breaks between phylums outward, they encapsulate their child designations). If you click on one of the phylum names, it zooms and a text box appears with a brief description and an image.


A Taxonomic Tree Ring

The Museum of Comparative Zoology (MCZ) is a private center for research and education focused on the comparative relationships of animal life. The MCZ houses millions of animal specimens divided into nine departments and hosts thousands of visiting researchers each year.
